# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

sendPUSH is for local business owners and operators evaluating direct customer messaging, loyalty, retention, and customer return campaigns. The primary audience includes restaurants, cafes, salons, clinics, retail shops, service providers, and other location-based businesses that need more predictable repeat visits without depending only on social media reach.

These users are usually comparing practical ways to bring customers back, fill quiet hours, recover lapsed visitors, and build a direct customer list. They need the website to feel clear, credible, and immediately relevant to daily business pressure, not abstract or software-first.

## Product Purpose

The website explains how sendPUSH and Loyaltymaster help businesses create direct customer relationships through digital loyalty cards, geo-aware push messaging, automated retention campaigns, and trial signup flows.

Success means a qualified local business understands the value quickly, trusts the offer, sees concrete use cases, and can move toward a trial or sales conversation without confusion.

## Brand Personality

The brand personality is confident local growth: practical, warm, credible, and conversion-focused.

The site should feel like a trustworthy growth partner for real businesses. It should speak plainly, show momentum, and make direct customer engagement feel achievable. It can be energetic and optimistic, but it should stay grounded in business outcomes rather than hype.

## Anti-references

Do not make future pages feel like generic SaaS templates. Avoid sterile blue or purple gradients, vague AI-looking layouts, endless same-sized feature-card grids, empty startup polish, and visual choices that could belong to any software company.

Avoid over-abstract product storytelling. The site should not feel like a dashboard vendor, a crypto landing page, a social media agency template, or a cold enterprise brochure.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with local business outcomes. Every page should connect the design and message to more visits, more repeat customers, stronger retention, or clearer customer ownership.
2. Show directness over abstraction. Prefer concrete scenarios, recognizable sections, and visible conversion paths over vague platform language.
3. Stay warm and credible. Use the existing friendly color system, tactile CTAs, and approachable spacing without becoming childish or gimmicky.
4. Preserve the built system. New pages should reuse the established section patterns, typography, CTA treatments, and responsive behavior from the current site.
5. Make action obvious. Primary CTAs, signup paths, and supporting proof should be easy to scan and keyboard accessible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Future website pages should target WCAG AA. Maintain responsive layouts across desktop, tablet, and mobile; preserve keyboard usability; keep visible focus states; respect reduced motion preferences; and keep text contrast at AA levels or better.

Do not rely on color alone to communicate meaning. Forms, CTAs, navigation, accordions, and interactive cards should have clear labels, accessible names, and predictable focus behavior.
